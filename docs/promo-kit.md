On the built-in benchmark, it lifted all tested backends from 33% to 100% without retraining weights.

### Reddit / X / LinkedIn post

I open-sourced AI-DNA, a runnable prototype for an evolvable AI architecture.

Instead of treating intelligence as one monolithic model, AI-DNA splits capability into codon-like units, composes them into genes, routes them through a genome, and improves behavior through mutation, crossover, selection, and epigenetic tuning.

The interesting part is that the current prototype improves small backends at the architecture layer, without retraining their weights. On the built-in benchmark, the tested backends move from 33% baseline accuracy to 100% once routed through AI-DNA.
